## Summary of Findings

### Key Strengths Across Licenses
1. **Environmental consciousness**: Strong in SUL, EIL, and CAL
2. **Transparency and accountability**: Present in all licenses
3. **Stakeholder engagement**: Well-addressed in SBL and CGL
4. **Governance structures**: Comprehensive in CGL
5. **Ethical AI considerations**: Detailed in EAL

### Critical Gaps

#### 1. **Mathematical Rigor (CFSA)**
- **Status**: Missing across all licenses
- **Impact**: No quantitative validation of alignment claims
- **BGI Requirement**: Jacobian-based sensitivity analysis, coherence metrics, determinant-based validation

#### 2. **Temporal Fidelity**
- **Status**: Weak across all licenses
- **Impact**: No synchronization with biological/ecological rhythms
- **BGI Requirement**: Seasonal calibration, ancestral accountability, temporal coherence verification

#### 3. **Neurodivergent Cognition Protocols**
- **Status**: Minimal coverage
- **Impact**: Missing cognitive rhythm adaptation and neurodivergent-specific considerations
- **BGI Requirement**: Circadian-aware protocols, cognitive state monitoring, LOVE-evolution checkpoints

#### 4. **Bioregional Grounding**
- **Status**: Partial in some licenses
- **Impact**: Environmental requirements are global, not location-specific
- **BGI Requirement**: Geolocation-bound optimization, local sensor network integration

#### 5. **Intergenerational Accountability**
- **Status**: Implicit at best
- **Impact**: Short-term focus without explicit long-term consequence tracking
- **BGI Requirement**: Decision consequences traceable across ecological timelines
