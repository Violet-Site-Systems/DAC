### 2. Climate Accountability Principles

All users of this software commit to:

a) **Climate Transparency**: Measure and publicly report the carbon footprint of software deployment and operations.

b) **Emissions Reduction**: Set and work toward science-based targets for reducing greenhouse gas emissions.

c) **Paris Agreement Alignment**: Ensure practices align with limiting global warming to 1.5Â°C above pre-industrial levels.

d) **Climate Justice**: Consider the disproportionate climate impacts on vulnerable communities.

### 3. Carbon Accounting Requirements

Licensees operating at significant scale must:

a) **Measure Carbon Footprint**: Calculate the carbon emissions from:

- Energy consumption (compute, storage, network)
  - Embedded emissions in hardware
  - Employee travel and operations
  - Supply chain emissions (Scope 3)

b) **Reporting Standards**: Use recognized standards such as the GHG Protocol or ISO 14064.

c) **Regular Disclosure**: Publish annual carbon footprint reports with methodology documentation.

d) **Verification**: For large deployments, obtain third-party verification of emissions data.

### 4. Reduction Commitments

Users must:

a) **Set Targets**: Establish concrete, time-bound emissions reduction targets.

b) **Reduction Plans**: Develop and implement plans to achieve reduction targets.

c) **Progress Tracking**: Monitor and report progress against targets annually.

d) **Continuous Improvement**: Regularly review and strengthen reduction commitments.

### 5. Clean Energy Requirements

a) **Renewable Energy**: Transition to 100% renewable energy for operations where feasible.

b) **Energy Efficiency**: Implement measures to improve energy efficiency of software operations.

c) **Green Procurement**: Prioritize suppliers and service providers with strong climate commitments.

d) **Infrastructure Choices**: Select data centers and cloud providers with renewable energy commitments.

### 6. Carbon Removal and Offsetting

a) **Hierarchy of Action**: Prioritize emissions reduction over offsetting.

b) **High-Quality Offsets**: If using carbon offsets, ensure they are:
- Additional (would not have happened otherwise)
   - Permanent (carbon stored long-term)
   - Verified by credible third parties
  - Avoid double-counting

c) **Offset Disclosure**: Clearly disclose the use of offsets and their quality criteria.

d) **Carbon Removal**: Support permanent carbon removal technologies and nature-based solutions.

### 7. Climate-Positive Design

Software design should:

a) **Optimize Efficiency**: Design for minimal computational resource consumption.

b) **Enable Climate Action**: Where possible, design features that support climate action and awareness.

c) **Avoid Rebound Effects**: Consider whether efficiency gains might lead to increased overall consumption.

d) **Lifecycle Thinking**: Design for long hardware lifecycles and minimal e-waste.

### 8. Climate Risk Adaptation

a) **Risk Assessment**: Assess climate-related risks to software operations and continuity.

b) **Resilience Planning**: Develop plans to ensure service continuity despite climate impacts.

c) **Community Impact**: Consider how climate adaptation measures affect vulnerable communities.

### 9. Advocacy and Influence

Licensees are encouraged to:

a) **Climate Advocacy**: Advocate for strong climate policies and regulations.

b) **Industry Leadership**: Share best practices and push industry standards forward.

c) **Ecosystem Influence**: Use procurement and partnership decisions to encourage climate action in the broader ecosystem.

### 10. Prohibited Uses

This software may not be used to:

a) **Climate Denial**: Support activities that deny or obscure climate science.

b) **Greenwashing**: Misrepresent environmental impact or climate action.

c) **Fossil Fuel Expansion**: Directly support expansion of fossil fuel extraction or infrastructure (existing operations transition support may be permitted).

d) **Environmental Harm**: Applications that directly contribute to deforestation, habitat destruction, or biodiversity loss.

### 11. Reporting and Transparency

Annual climate reports should include:

- Total GHG emissions (Scope 1, 2, and 3 where material)
- Emissions intensity metrics
- Progress toward reduction targets
- Renewable energy percentage
- Climate-related investments and initiatives
- Methodology and assumptions

### 12. Small Deployment Exception

Individual developers and small-scale deployments are exempt from formal reporting requirements but are encouraged to apply climate-conscious practices.
